Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common pet care questions

How often should I take my pet for a vet check-up?
Puppies and kittens should visit the vet every 3-4 weeks until they are about 4 months old. Adult pets should have at least one annual check-up, while senior pets (7+ years) benefit from bi-annual visits. Regular check-ups help catch potential health issues early.
What vaccinations does my pet need?
Core vaccinations for dogs include rabies, distemper, parvovirus, and adenovirus. For cats: rabies, feline distemper, calicivirus, and herpesvirus. Non-core vaccines depend on lifestyle and location. Always consult with your veterinarian for a personalized vaccination schedule.
How can I tell if my pet is in pain?
Signs of pain in pets include decreased activity, limping, difficulty standing up, changes in eating or drinking habits, vocalizing more than usual, aggression, or hiding. Cats often become quieter and may stop grooming. If you suspect your pet is in pain, contact your veterinarian immediately.
What human foods are dangerous for pets?
Never feed pets: chocolate, grapes/raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol (found in sugar-free products), alcohol, caffeine, avocado, macadamia nuts, or raw yeast dough. Some human foods like cooked plain meat or vegetables may be safe in moderation, but always consult your vet first.
